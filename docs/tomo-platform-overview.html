1 What is Tomo?

Tomo is an AI-powered coaching platform built for young athletes (ages 13-25). It combines daily wellness tracking, intelligent scheduling, personalized training programs, and a conversational AI coach into a single app that adapts to your life -- balancing sport, school, recovery, and personal growth.

At its core, Tomo does three things:

Understands You

Every check-in, test, session, and wearable reading builds a real-time picture of your readiness, load, and development trajectory.

Advises You

AI-powered recommendations tell you what to train, when to rest, and how to balance academics -- personalized to your sport, position, age, and current state.

Protects You

9 safety guardrails automatically cap training load, block risky exercises during growth spurts, and flag injury risk before problems happen.

Grows With You

Longitudinal tracking of speed, strength, endurance, agility, and mastery pillars builds your athlete identity and professional CV over time.

3 Your Daily Journey

Here is what a typical day looks like for a Tomo athlete, from waking up to winding down.

3.1 Morning: Open the App

When you open Tomo, the app loads your entire state in one shot. Behind the scenes, 18 parallel queries run to fetch your profile, snapshot, schedule, check-in history, recommendations, benchmarks, wearable data, and more. This takes under 2 seconds.

You land on the Dashboard -- your daily command centre. At the top, you see your Athlete Mode (Balanced, League, Study, or Rest) and the readiness signal derived from yesterday's data.

If you haven't checked in yet

The dashboard shows a BASELINE signal (grey) and the check-in button in the header shows a red dot. Your first action should always be to check in.

3.2 Check In

The daily check-in is a 7-step wellness wizard that takes about 30 seconds:

StepWhat You RateHow
1Sleep qualityEmoji scale (1-10)
2Energy levelEmoji scale (1-10)
3Stress levelEmoji scale (1-10)
4SorenessEmoji scale (1-10)
5Pain flagYes / No (safety trigger)
6MoodEmoji scale (1-10)
7MotivationEmoji scale (1-10)

Tapping an emoji auto-advances to the next step. On completion, a confetti animation plays and your readiness score is computed instantly:

Green -- Good to Go

Full training is safe. All systems are recovered. Train at full intensity.

Yellow -- Proceed with Care

Some stress markers are elevated. Training is fine but high-intensity work should be moderated.

Red -- Recovery Priority

Multiple markers are suppressed. High-intensity training is blocked. Focus on mobility, light movement, and recovery.

Pain Flag Safety Gate

If you report pain at step 5, Tomo immediately triggers a REST recommendation and flags injury risk. Your readiness drops to RED regardless of other scores, and high-intensity programs are blocked until the next clean check-in.

3.3 Dashboard -- Your Command Centre

After check-in, the Dashboard updates in real-time. It is built from CMS-managed sections that your coach or performance director can reorder, enable, or disable. The standard layout has four zones:

ZoneSectionWhat You See
1Athlete Mode HeroCurrent mode pill (Balanced/League/Study/Rest), quick-switch pills, signal badge
2Daily RecommendationsPriority-sorted recommendation cards (expandable): readiness, load warnings, recovery, development, academic, motivation
3CMS SectionsWelcome card, protocol alerts, growth tracking, readiness ring, core vitals (KPI row), weekly trend sparklines, dual load index, benchmark progress, AI recommendations, upcoming events, consistency streak bar
4Up NextYour next scheduled activities with contextual AI hints

The Readiness Ring

A circular animated ring shows your readiness score (0-100). Green ring for scores above 70, yellow for 40-70, red below 40. Below the ring, a trend indicator shows how today compares to yesterday (e.g. "+8 vs yesterday").

Core Vitals Row

Compact KPI tiles showing your key numbers at a glance: sleep hours, HRV, energy, soreness, ACWR, and dual load index. Each tile includes a sparkline showing the 7-day trend.

3.4 Plan Your Day

Switch to the Plan tab to see your full daily timeline. Events are laid out vertically by time:

  • School blocks -- locked, shown in grey, cannot be moved
  • Training sessions -- club, gym, or personal, with intensity badges
  • Study blocks -- scheduled study time with subject tags
  • Matches -- fixture events with pre/post buffers
  • Recovery -- auto-generated after matches or when readiness is RED

Tap any event to edit it. Tap a gap in the timeline to add a new event. Use the floating action button (+) to quickly create training, study, or other blocks.

Smart Conflict Detection

If you try to schedule a HARD session the day before a match, or squeeze two sessions into a time slot that violates your buffer rules, Tomo flags the conflict and suggests alternative times.

3.5 Train

When it is time to train, Tomo can build you a personalized session. Ask Tomo Chat "build me a session" or tap the training block on your timeline. The AI considers your readiness, position, benchmark gaps, and current load to select drills and set appropriate volumes.

During a drill, the Drill Detail screen provides a guided timer: countdown to start, active phase (counting down each set), rest phases between sets, and a completion screen where you log touches, reaction times, and RPE.

3.6 Study

Tomo treats academics as seriously as training. The exam planner lets you input exam dates and subjects, and Tomo generates a study schedule that respects your training load. In Study Mode, study blocks are extended by 50% and training intensity is capped on exam days.

3.7 Reflect & Wind Down

After sessions, Tomo may nudge you to log feedback (workout quality, fatigue level, notes). Journal entries build your coaching profile and contribute to your CV narrative. The notification engine tracks whether you have reflected and gently prompts you if you have not.

6 Intelligent Systems Behind the Scenes

You do not see these systems directly, but they power every recommendation, every safety guard, and every personalized response you receive.

6.1 Readiness Engine

Your readiness is computed from your daily check-in, wearable data (if connected), and historical trends. The engine produces a score (0-100) and a RAG status (Red/Amber/Green) that determines what training is safe today.

Check-in (7 inputs) + Wearable data (HRV, sleep, HR)
    --> Readiness Score (0-100)
    --> RAG Status: GREEN (≥70) | YELLOW (40-69) | RED (<40)
    --> Intensity Recommendation: HARD | MODERATE | LIGHT | REST

6.2 Schedule Rule Engine

A pure rules engine that governs all scheduling decisions. It uses a strict priority order:

PriorityCategoryNotes
P1SchoolNon-negotiable, locked blocks
P1ExamsNon-negotiable, locked blocks
P2MatchLeague/fixture, time set by club
P3RecoveryMandatory post-match or RED readiness
P4Club TrainingCoach-set times
P5GymIndividual strength & conditioning
P6StudyDaily academic block
P7Personal DevFilm study, tactical review, mental skills

Hard constraints enforced at all times:

  • Maximum 3 HARD sessions per week (adjustable by mode)
  • Maximum 2 sessions per day
  • No HARD training the day before a match
  • No HARD training on exam days
  • Mandatory recovery day after matches
  • 30-minute default buffer between events; 45 min after high intensity; 120 min before a match

6.3 Recommendation Intelligence Engine (RIE)

Nine specialized "computers" generate personalized recommendations:

ComputerWhat It Produces
ReadinessEnergy/soreness/sleep analysis, recovery urgency
RecoverySleep debt detection, HRV suppression alerts, soreness trend warnings
Load WarningACWR monitoring, dual load detection, overtraining risk
AcademicExam proximity alerts, study planning guidance

Triangle AlertCombined readiness + load + wellness risk flags
CV OpportunityPerformance highlights, personal records, recruitment narrative
Journal NudgePre/post-session reflection prompts

Recommendations are priority-sorted (P1 = critical, P4 = informational) and filtered by your athlete mode. In Study mode, academic recommendations are boosted to the top; in League mode, recovery recommendations take priority.

6.4 Program Guardrails (9 Safety Rules)

Every training program you see has passed through 9 deterministic safety checks:

#RuleWhat It Does
1ACWR Load GateIf your training load ratio is dangerously high (>1.5), caps volume at 60%
2Readiness GateRED readiness caps load at 50% and blocks explosive/HARD categories
3HRV SuppressionIf HRV is <85% of your baseline, reduces load proportionally
4Sleep QualityPoor sleep (<24h ago) reduces training volume by 20%
5Dual LoadHigh combined academic + athletic load caps training at 70%
6Injury RiskHigh injury risk: 60% load cap + injury prevention becomes mandatory
7Wellness TrendDeclining wellness over 7 days triggers volume reduction
8Training AgeBeginners (<12 weeks) get 60% load cap and blocked from advanced exercises
9Athlete ModeMode-specific load cap (rest mode = 50%) and category blocking
These rules cannot be overridden

Guardrails are applied after program selection (whether by AI or by a coach). Even if the AI recommends a high-intensity program, the guardrails will automatically reduce volumes or block it entirely if your current state makes it unsafe.

6.5 Protocol Layer (PDIL)

The Performance Director Intelligence Layer evaluates CMS-authored "protocols" against your current state. Protocols are condition-based rules that your coach or performance director sets up -- for example:

  • "If ACWR > 1.3 AND readiness is RED, enforce REST-only training and notify athlete"
  • "If PHV stage is mid-PHV, block barbell squats and Olympic lifts"
  • "If exam_proximity_score > 80, cap intensity at LIGHT"

Protocols can be mandatory (cannot train at all until resolved), advisory (training allowed with modifications), or informational. Safety-critical protocols force the AI to use a more careful model for responses.

6.6 Notification Engine

Notifications are generated automatically by event handlers and delivered to your notification centre. Key features:

  • 7 categories: Critical, Readiness, Recovery, Load Management, Achievements, Recommendations, Journal
  • Deduplication: Same alert type is updated, not duplicated (no "10 readiness RED" spam)
  • Fatigue guard: If you dismiss a notification type 3 times in 24 hours, Tomo stops sending it
  • Auto-expiry: Notifications resolve when the underlying condition changes (ACWR drops, readiness improves)
  • Actionable: Each notification has a primary action (e.g. "Log check-in", "See program", "Review recovery plan")

7 AI Chat -- How Tomo Understands You

Tomo Chat is not a generic chatbot. It is a three-agent system grounded in your real-time data, schedule rules, and performance context.

How a Message is Processed

You type a message
  --> Layer 1: Exact match (~170 phrases, instant, $0)
  --> Layer 2: Haiku AI classification (~60 intents, ~200ms)
  --> Layer 3: Full orchestrator with tools (complex queries)
  --> Route to Timeline / Output / Mastery agent
  --> Agent calls tools (get_readiness, create_event, log_test...)
  --> Format response (headline + stat cards, no wall of text)
  --> Stream back to you

What Tomo Knows When Responding

Every time Tomo responds, the AI has access to:

  • Your sport, position, age band, and gender
  • Current readiness score and RAG status
  • Today's schedule and upcoming events (7 days)
  • ACWR and load metrics
  • Benchmark percentiles, strengths, and gaps
  • Active recommendations from the RIE
  • Your schedule rules and available hours
  • Your athlete mode and its parameters
  • Active PDIL protocols and safety flags
  • Wearable data (HRV, sleep, heart rate)
  • Conversation history from the current session

Safety in Chat

  • Write actions require confirmation: Creating events, logging check-ins, and saving tests show a preview card. You must confirm before anything is saved.
  • RED readiness blocks intensity: If you ask to build a HARD session while RED, Tomo will refuse and suggest recovery activities instead.

9 Programs & Training

Tomo's training program system combines a 31-program catalog with AI-powered personalisation and deterministic safety guardrails.

How Programs Are Selected

1. AI analyses your full context (tests, vitals, PHV, readiness, load, position)
2. Selects 8-15 best-fit programs from 31-program catalogue
3. Assigns priority: mandatory (3-5), high (3-5), medium (2-5)
4. Personalises impact statements using YOUR data
5. 9 guardrail rules applied -- volumes adjusted, unsafe programs blocked
6. Final list cached for 24 hours, shown in Programs panel

Program Categories

  • Speed and acceleration
  • Strength and conditioning
  • Agility and change of direction
  • Plyometric and explosive power
  • Endurance and aerobic base
  • Injury prevention (Nordic, ACL, ankle, groin, hip)
  • Mobility and flexibility
  • Position-specific drills
  • Match preparation
  • Recovery and deload

Position Matrix

Each position has a training matrix defining mandatory programs (must do), recommended programs (should do), and weekly structure (how many sessions of each type per week). For example, a football winger has mandatory speed/acceleration work but lower strength emphasis than a centre-back.

Coach-Assigned Programmes

Your coach can publish programmes targeted at you specifically, your position group, or all athletes. These appear alongside AI-recommended programs but are marked as coach-assigned. The same 9 guardrails still apply.

13 How Everything Connects

Tomo is not a collection of separate features. Every action you take triggers a chain of updates that ripple across the entire platform. Here is how a single event flows through the system.

Example: You Complete a Training Session

1. You log the session (via Chat or Timeline)
    --> Event handler processes SESSION_LOG
    --> Daily load updated (ACWR recalculated)
    --> Session count and training age updated
    --> Athlete snapshot (Layer 2) rewritten

2. Notifications fire (async)
    --> If ACWR now > 1.3: "Load Warning" notification created
    --> Journal nudge: "How did the session go?"

3. Next time you open the app (boot)
    --> Fresh snapshot loaded
    --> PDIL re-evaluates protocols against new load
    --> Guardrails update: future programs adjusted for ACWR
    --> Signal evaluates: load warning pill may appear on dashboard
    --> Recommendations re-sorted by new priorities

4. You ask Tomo Chat "am I overtraining?"
    --> Agent sees updated ACWR in context
    --> Responds with load analysis + recovery recommendation
    --> Suggests deload if ACWR is in danger zone

Example: You Switch to Study Mode

1. You tap "Study" on the mode selector
    --> PATCH to schedule preferences
    --> Mode change event processed
    --> Snapshot updated with new mode
    --> Dashboard refreshes instantly (optimistic UI)

2. All downstream systems cascade
    --> Schedule rules: study blocks extend 50%, gym days reduce
    --> Validation: new events checked against study mode caps
    --> AI: coaching tone shifts to "academic", study-focused
    --> Guardrails: load cap drops to 70%
    --> Programs: AI selects lighter programs, blocks plyometrics
    --> Recommendations: ACADEMIC recs boosted to top of list

The Athlete Snapshot -- Single Source of Truth

At the centre of everything is the athlete snapshot -- a single database row containing ~80 computed fields that represent your current state. Every event (check-in, session, test, wearable sync) updates the snapshot. Every downstream system (guardrails, recommendations, signals, AI, protocols) reads from it.

This means there is no stale data, no conflicting views, and no system that "doesn't know" about your latest check-in. One truth, everywhere, always current.

The Safety Promise

If anything goes wrong in the system (a database query fails, the AI service is unreachable, a protocol evaluation errors out), Tomo defaults to the most conservative option: lower load caps, stricter intensity limits, and supportive coaching. The system is designed to fail safe, never fail dangerous.
